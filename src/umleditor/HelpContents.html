Introduction

The UML Editor is designed to create, edit, open, and save UML class diagrams. The program is optimized for Windows 7 and assumes that you have Java 6 (update 20 or higher) installed. In these help contents, all "click" instructions refer to a left click. Any right clicks will be specifically mentioned. Instructions such as File>Save indicate that you should select "File" from the menu bar and the "Save" option will be an item in the File menu. Commands such as Ctrl+C indicate that you should press the indciated keyboard buttons (Ctrl and C in this case) at the same time.

    • Limitations

    UML Editor has the following limitations. First, only class diagrams may be added. Other types of UML diagrams are not supported. In addition, each diagram can only hold a maximum of 500 classes. Another limtation is that labels and cardinality information for relationships is not included.

    Classes

    To add a class, click on the Add Class button (or press N on your keyboard). Then click inside the UML Editor window to create the class. To create multiple classes, hold down Shift on your keyboard while clicking inside the UML Editor window. New classes may also be added by right clicking in any open area inside the Editor window. In the context menu that appears, you may choose "Add Class" to add a class where the right click occured.

    To select a single class, simply click on it. The class will be highlighted to indicate that it was selected. To select multiple classes, hold down the Ctrl button on your keyboard while clicking on classes. As you select multiple classes each one will be highlighted to indicate that it was selected.

    To edit a class, double click on the class to launch the Edit Class window. You can modify the class name, attributes or methods. To add attributes or methods, fill out the blank field in each section and then press the New button. All fields will be updated in real time. To remove any changes made, press the Discard Changes. To confirm changes and close the window, press the Close button. You may also access the Edit Class window by right clicking on any class and choosing "Edit."

    To move a class, hover your cursor over a class's name field. The cursor will change to the move symbol. Click down and drag the class to a new location and then release your click. Any relationships associated with the class will also be moved with the class. Multiple classes may be moved by using the same method. Simply select more than one class and then move your cursor over one of the selected classes. When you click and drag to move the class, all highlighted classes and any associated relationships are moved as well.

    To delete a class, click on a class to select it. The Delete Button in the toolbar will become active. Click on the Delete button (or press the Del key on your keyboard) and the class will be deleted along with any relationships to/from the class. You may also delete a class by right clicking on the class and choosing "Delete." To delete multiple classes, simply select any classes you wish to delete and then either press the Delete button on the toolbar or press the Del key on your keyboard.

    To cut, copy, and paste classes, first select a class. To cut, press Ctrl+X or choose Edit>Cut from the menu bar. To copy, press Ctrl+C or choose Edit>Copy from the menu bar. You may also Cut and Copy by right clicking on a class and choosing "Cut" or "Copy" from the context menu. To paste, press Ctrl+V, right click on an open area and choose "Paste," or choose Edit>Paste from the menu bar. When using the keyboard shortcuts and right click menu, the pasted classes will appear next to your mouse cursor. When pasting from the menu bar, pasted classes will appear in the center of the screen.

    Relationships

    To create a relationship, first ensure that two classes have been created. Then, using your mouse, click inside your first class (without the move symbol activated). Drag your mouse and release your click when your cursor is on the second class. You will see a line while you drag your mouse. After releasing your click on the second class, the Relationship Chooser window will appear. Choose which type of relationship you want (composition, aggregation, dependency, association, and generalization). The selected relationship will be drawn between the two classes.

    To shape a relationship, click on a relationship to select it. Two endpoint, blue nodes will appear. Click and drag on an endpoint to move it around the class. To create a new node along the relationship's path, Ctrl+click anywhere on the relationship (or right click on the relationship and choose "Create Node"). Then click and drag this node to change the shape of the relationship.

    To edit a relationship, double click on the relationship or right click on the relationship and choose "Edit." The Edit Relationship window will appear. You may choose which new type of relationship you would like drawn or change the direction of the relationship.

    To delete a relationship node, click on the node to select it (the node will turn red), then press the Delete button on the toolbar (or the Del key on your keyboard) to delete the node. The relationship will then redraw itself. Endpoint nodes may not be deleted.

    To delete a relationship, click on a relationship to select it. Then press the Delete button on the toolbar (or the Delete key on your keyboard) to delete the relationship. A relationship may also be deleted by right clicking on the relationship and choosing "Delete."

    Loading & Saving UML Diagrams

    To save an unsaved UML diagram, choose File>Save from the menu bar (or press CTRL+S on your keyboard). A Save window will appear, allowing you to choose the name and location of your UML diagram. Choosing File>Save (or pressing Ctrl+S) will update the saved file. To explicitly save to a different location, choose File>Save As from the file menu. After saving a diagram, the diagram's name will serve as a title for the diagram in the tab bar. In addition, any changes to a saved diagram will result in an asterisk (*) next to the tab's title to indicate that there are changes that need to be saved.

    To load a saved UML diagram, choose File>Load from the menu bar (or press Ctrl+O on your keyboard). A Load window will appear, allowing you to navigate to your saved diagram and load it. The loaded diagram will appear in a new tab.

    Printing

    To print the UML diagram of your current tab, choose File>Print from the menu bar (or press Ctrl+P on your keyboard). A Print window will appear. You may choose your printer, the number of copies you would like to print, and a portrait or landscape orientation in addition to other options. To send the diagram to the printer, choose "Print." To cancel the print job, press "Cancel." The diagram will be printed on one page, with font size of the classes resized depending on the size of diagram.
